5-Day Brain Boost Plan

5-Day STEAM Exploration Camp

  • 3. Lava & Vinegar Volcano

  • 1. Solids, Liquids & Gases Adventure
    • Activity: Hands-on demo‚Äîfeel ice (solid), touch water (liquid), see water vapor with boiling kettle (gas).
    • Concepts: Properties of matter
  • 2. Fizzing Balloons (Vinegar Rocket)
    • Activity: Fill a balloon with baking soda, fit over bottle with vinegar, watch it puff up with gas!
    • Concepts: Gas production, reactions

Day 3: Reaching for the Stars ‚Äì Astronomy Day

  • 1. Planet Coloring Book
    • Activity: Kids color the planets and learn fun facts ("Which planet has the most rings?" "What color is Mars?")
    • Supplies: Printable coloring pages
  • 2. How the Solar System Began
    • Activity: Storytime: How did the solar system start? Kids act out the "swirling cloud" with scarves.
    • Concepts: Planets formed from dust and gas; everything circles the sun

Day 4: Fantastic Physics Day

    • Activity: Build soft "cannons" with Nerf guns or balloon launchers. Shoot at different angles/strengths, chart results.
    • Concepts: Vectors, aiming, basic physics of motion
  • 2. Hot Wheels: Distance vs. Displacement
    • Activity: Build twisty and straight tracks. Measure track length (distance) and the straight line from start to finish (displacement).

Day 5: Incredible Engineers

  • 1. Toy Plane Challenge

Week 4
STEAM Explorers Week

5-Day Advanced STEAM Exploration Camp

Day 1: Advanced Biology & Chemistry

  • 1. Strawberry DNA Extraction
    • Activity: Students extract DNA from strawberries using dish soap, salt, and alcohol, observing DNA strands.
    • Concepts: Introduction to genetics, DNA structure, why DNA extraction matters.
  • 2. Photosynthesis Inquiry
    • Activity: Use aquatic plants (like Elodea) in different light conditions. Measure oxygen output with bubbles or gas sensors.
    • Concepts: Role of sunlight in energy conversion, designing controlled scientific investigations.
  • 3. Erupting Lava Volcano (with Redox Twist)
    • Activity: Create a "volcano" with baking soda, vinegar, and discuss the acid-base reaction and products formed.
    • Concepts: Chemical reactions, chemical equations, pH scale.

Day 2: Powerful Chemistry

  • 1. Acid-Base Neutralization Lab
    • Activity: Perform titrations with vinegar (acid) and baking soda (base), using universal indicator or red cabbage juice for pH visualization.
    • Concepts: Acids and bases, neutralization reactions, indicators, measuring pH.
  • 2. Fizzing Balloons (Limiting Reactant Challenge)
    • Activity: Teams experiment with different vinegar and baking soda amounts to fill balloons optimally; relate observed results to chemical equations.
    • Concepts: Chemical stoichiometry, gas production, experimental design.

Day 3: Astronomy & Exoplanet Discovery

  • 1. How Are Habitable Planets Found?
    • Lesson & Activity: Interactive demo: Simulate planet detection using the transit and radial velocity methods with flashlights and balls. Discuss the "habitable zone" and what makes a planet possibly life-friendly.
    • Concepts: Exoplanet detection, "Goldilocks Zone," interpreting real exoplanet data.
  • 2. Create an Exoplanet Profile
    • Activity: Design an imaginary planet that's habitable‚Äîdecide its size, orbit, star type, and key features. Present to the group.
    • Concepts: Application of astronomy, evidence-based thinking

Day 4: Physics‚ÄîMotion & Vectors

  • 1. Nerf Gun Launchers (Intro to Vectors)
    • Activity: Use Nerf darts or ping-pong launchers. Vary the angle and measure the distance. Chart angle vs. range. Connect to basic vector equations.
    • Concepts: Vector addition, projectile motion, graphing results.
  • 2. Hot Wheels & Circular Motion Lab
    • Activity: Build circular Hot Wheels tracks (loops). Investigate what speeds allow cars to complete the loop (centripetal force). Experiment with different heights and car masses.
    • Concepts: Circular motion, forces, acceleration, minimum speed for loop.
  • 3. Discussion: Distance vs. Displacement (connect to motion around loops and launches).

Day 5: Engineering Design Challenges

  • 1. Toy Plane Distance Challenge
    • Activity: Build gliders (balsa/paper/foam). Test flights; use stopwatches and tape measures; modify for best range/stability; graph results.
    • Concepts: Engineering design, aerodynamics, variables in testing.
  • 2. Marshmallow & Spaghetti Tower Competition
    • Activity: Teams build the tallest and/or strongest towers with given materials. Test against simulated wind (fan or shaking table).
    • Concepts: Structural engineering, teamwork, stability vs. height, rapid prototyping.
